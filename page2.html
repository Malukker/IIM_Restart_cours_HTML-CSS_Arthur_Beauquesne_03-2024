ALBUMS

Sounding The Seventh Trumpet

Metalcore

First ever released album of the band, published 2001 at Good Life Recordings then 2002 at Hopeless Records. Is not considered as good as the following ones by most fans but will certainly content most Metalcore enjoyers. Most of the songs are screamed. Contains a lot of religious references.

Waking The Fallen

Metalcore

Second album of the band, published 2003 at Hopeless Records. Is way more mature in its song writing, featuring as much scream as plain singing. First real success of the band, peaking at 10th place in the Billboard 200 and getting certified platinum in the US.

City Of Evil

Heavy Metal

Published 2005 at Warner Records and Hopeless Records. Peaked at 30th place in Billboard 200 and was certified platinum. Does not feature any screaming, as the place is given to guitar solos, riffs and more lyrical voices. Has much more of a Heavy Metal style, with Power Metal influences. Is considered the album that made the band known on an international scale.

Avenged Sevenfold

Heavy Metal

Eponymous album, published 2007 at Warner Records and Hopeless Records. Placed 4th in Billboard 200, 12th in Oricon and 24 in OCC, and was certified platinum. Won the Kerrang! Album Of The Year award. Although being mostly Heavy Metal, its songs have lots of different styles, would it be more symphonic, country or experimental styles. That abundance of styles and influences has long since become the band's signature way of writing songs. An overall extremely strong album.

Nightmare

Heavy Metal

Published 2010 at Warner Records. Placed 1st in Billboard 200, 5th in OCC, 12th in Oricon, was certified platinum, has won the Golden Gods awards for best drummer (twice), guitarists, vocalist, and album of the year. Started its writing normally, but changed a lot with the death of the band's drummer, Jimmy "The Rev" Sullivan, in December 2009. The lyrics from all songs mention more or less explicitly his death and the loss it represented to the band, and the overall songwriting style is very grim.

Hail To The King

Heavy Metal

Published 2013 at Warner Records. Placed 1st in Billboard 200, 1st in OCC, 5th in Germany, 8th in Italy, 12th in Spain, 15th in Japan, 25th in France, was certified platinum twice, haw won the Loudwire Best Rock Song award, and the Golden Gods awards for Best guitarists and best singer. It is all about heavy metal and is very classic in its style, which can explain its success on an international scale. It is the most successful album they ever released, but also the one they consider the best out of their whole discography.

The Stage

Prog Metal

Published 2016 at Capitol Records. Placed 4th in Billboard 200 and 13 in OCC. Is themed around the potential danger AI could represent, and society's self-destruction. Is the band's first concept album, and is very progressive and melodic in its style. The songs are all very mystical in the way they sound. As a concept album, it did not sell really well, although it was praised by the critics.

Life Is But A Dream...

Prog Metal

Latest album from the band. Published 2023 at Warner Records. Placed 13th in Billboard 200 and 21st in OCC. Second concept album of the band, it's themed around absurdism and existentialism. It uses a number of electronic effects through all its different songs, and is generally very avant-garde and progressive for metal standards. It has received a pretty unanimous critical acclaim, which can be explained by the fact it's a very niche album.
